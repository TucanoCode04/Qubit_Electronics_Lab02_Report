\section{Setup}
The device, showed in figure \ref{passband},  has two ports which have been
connected to the VNA ports. By looking at the device's 
response, we focused on the range 1.5GHz-3GHz, which is the one worth to be analyzed. The results have been extracted
as Touchstone files and then they have been elaborated using python, in particular using the \texttt{scikit-rf} module.

\section{Analysis of the measurements}
By looking at figure \ref{plots_full}, which shows the magnitude of the signal of the respective channel, is it clear that the device has a reciprocal response
between the two ports, since the plots of $S_{11}$,$S_{22}$ are identical as well as $S_{12}$,$S_{21}$ plots. The validity of this assumption is confirmed by the extraced values showed in table \ref{tab_pb}.
This is a typical behavior of a passive component and so, for the rest of the analysis, we will focus only on the $S_{22}$ and $S_{12}$ parameters (second row of the figure).

By examinating the $S_{12}$ parameter, we understand that the device is allowing the signal to pass from input port (port 2) to output port (port 1) in a frequency range around $2275$MHz,
with a bandwidth of $123.31$MHz. This tells us that the device has a quite wide bandwidth, making it suitable for the transmission of a not so limited number of frequencies. At peak frequency, the signal has an attenuation of $-4.88$dB, meaning that when the transmitted signal is at its peak, almost 60\% of it is transmitted. This 
is due to the fact that the device is not an ideal one so it is affected by internal losses.
Regarding the $S_{22}$ parameter, we observe that the signal is almost completely reflected at frequencies below $2200$MHz and above $2300$MHz,
while the reflection is minimum between these values at $2275$MHz where the magnitude of the reflected signal corresponds to $-19.53$dB. This behaviour is consistent with the previous analysis of the $S_{12}$ parameter,
since the signal is transmitted to port 1 from port 2 at the same frequency range at wich, on port 2, the signal is not reflected.

\section{Summary}
In conclusion, we can state that the device under test acts as a two-ports reciprocal passband filter. This conclusion is supported by the transmission bandwidth ($S_{12}$ or $S_{21}$), which is sufficiently wide to exclude the possibility of a high-Q resonator.
\chapter{Antenna}
\section{Setup}
The DUT is shown in figure \ref{antenna}. It has a single port which has been connected to the VNA port (port 1 in this case).
Since in this case we have only one port,
the study will be focused on the $S_{11}$ parameter, which is the only one available, and so on the behavior of the reflected signal.
\begin{figure}[H]
    \includegraphics[width=0.5\textwidth]{leopart/antenna.png}
    \centering
    \caption{Image of the device connected to the VNA}
    \label{antenna}
\end{figure}
\section{Analysis of the measurements}
From the plot illustrated in figure \ref{antenna_plot} we can observe two dips in the 
magnitude of the reflected signal at a frequencies of $f_{res} = 3001.89$ MHz and $f_{res} = 6058.68$ MHz. At this frequencies, the signal
has a return loss of $-11.46$dB and $-16.34$dB respectively, with a matching bandwidth of $BW = 6.49$ MHz and $BW = 84.37$ MHz (calculated at $<-10$ dB threshold).
When we reach frequencies around $3500$ MHz, the measurements show an unstable behavior where the magnitude exceeds $0$ dB. This represents an "unphysical gain" and is likely a calibration artifact or the result of a poor connection between the device and the VNA port during the setup phase. 
\\Focussing now on the operating frequenices of the device, which comprehends signals
around $300$ and $6000$ MHz, the return losses at these resonance frequencies indicate that $92.8\%$ of the incident signal in the first case, and $97.67\%$ in the second case, is not reflected, but instead
is transmitted by the device. Since we have only one port, we can assume that the signal is transmitted by irradiation in the space, meaning that the device is acting like an antenna at the resonance frequency of $3001.89$ and $6058.68$ MHz. \\
By considering the matching bandwiths of $6.49$  and $84.37$ MHz, which leads to a very high quality factors of $Q = 462.5$ and $Q = 71.4$, we can state that the device has a high selectivity on the transmitted frequencies but also a very low
sensitivity to the variations of the frequency of the transmitted signal. This makes the DUT also not too reliable, since a small variation also due to external noise can lead to a significant decrease of the transmitted signal.

    \includegraphics[width=0.8\textwidth]{leopart/antenna_plot.png}
    \centering
    \caption{Plot of the $S_{11}$ parameter of S matrix}
    \label{antenna_plot}
\end{figure}
\section{Summary}
In conclusion, the DUT in this case acts as a single-port dual-band antenna which transmits signals at a frequency of around $3000$ and $6000$ MHz with a very low matching bandwidths and so a high selectivity of the transmitted frequencies.
An interesting observation is that if a hand is placed close to the surface of the device, we could see a shift of the dip towards lower frequencies. This phenomenon is due to the capacitive coupling
between the hand and the device, which affects the resonant frequency of the antenna.
